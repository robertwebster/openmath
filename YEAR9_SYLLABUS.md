# Year 9 NSW Mathematics — Syllabus Map

**Syllabus:** Mathematics K–10 (2022), implemented in Year 9 from 2024  
**Stage:** Stage 5 (Years 9–10)  
**Structure:** Core (mandatory) + Paths (extension, noted separately)

> ⚠️ **Verification note:** Exact NESA outcome codes (e.g. `MA5-XX-01`) should be confirmed against the official digital curriculum at [curriculum.nsw.edu.au](https://curriculum.nsw.edu.au/learning-areas/mathematics/mathematics-k-10-2022/outcomes) before content is published. The NESA site requires JavaScript to render outcome detail.

---

## Navigation and Quiz Structure

The content is organised into **two navigable tiers** — Topic and Sub-topic. Strand is metadata only (used for filtering/tagging, not navigation).

Three quiz levels exist, all drawing from a **single source of truth** at the sub-topic level:

| Level | What it covers | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **Sub-topic quiz** | One specific concept | "I need to practise finding unknown angles" |
| **Topic quiz** | All sub-topics in a topic | "I think I've got Trigonometry — test me" |
| **Year 9 quiz** | All topics in Year 9 | "I have an exam on everything" |

Topic and Year 9 quizzes are aggregated automatically from sub-topic question banks. Questions are tagged at sub-topic level — no separate question banks to maintain at higher levels.

## Year 9 Topics

---

### Trigonometry
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Pythagoras and trigonometry — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Sin, Cos and Tan ratios | Identifying opposite, adjacent, hypotenuse; SOHCAHTOA |
| Finding unknown sides | Applying trig ratios to solve for missing sides |
| Finding unknown angles | Using inverse trig ratios (sin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, tan⁻¹) |
| Angles of elevation and depression | Real-world trig problems, drawing diagrams |

**Open Math notes:** Highest priority topic. Biggest new concept in Year 9, highest student anxiety. Visual — sub-topic pages should include labelled triangle diagrams. Consider a custom renderer for the topic landing page.

---

### Pythagoras' Theorem
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Pythagoras and trigonometry — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Finding the hypotenuse | a² + b² = c², applying the formula |
| Finding a shorter side | Rearranging the formula |
| Pythagorean triads | Recognising common triples (3-4-5, 5-12-13) |
| Applications | Real-world problems in 2D |

**Open Math notes:** Often taught before Trigonometry. Foundational — students who struggle with Pythagoras will struggle with Trig. Good confidence-builder as questions have clear right/wrong answers with a single formula.

---

### Equations and Algebra
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Algebra and equations — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Solving multi-step equations | Balancing equations, inverse operations |
| Expanding expressions | Distributive law, binomial products |
| Factorising expressions | Common factors, simple trinomials |
| Rearranging formulas | Making a variable the subject |

**Open Math notes:** Foundational to everything else in Year 9. A student who can't solve equations will struggle across the board. Good first topic to build — no custom renderer needed.

---

### Index Laws
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Algebra and equations / Multiplicative relationships — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Index law rules | Product rule, quotient rule, power of a power |
| Zero and negative indices | Why a⁰ = 1, expressing with positive indices |
| Scientific notation | Converting, comparing, and operating with very large/small numbers |

**Open Math notes:** Contained, learnable, good for quick wins. Students can feel confident after a short focused practice session. Default renderer.

---

### Financial Mathematics
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Number and finance — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Simple interest | I = Prn, finding I, P, r and n |
| Compound interest | A = P(1 + r)ⁿ, comparing with simple interest |
| Earning money | Wages, salaries, overtime, tax basics |
| Budgeting and applications | Income, expenses, percentage change |

**Open Math notes:** Real-world relevance makes this one of the most engaging topics. Strong confidence-builder — students can see why it matters. Scenario-based question framing recommended.

---

### Linear Relationships
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Linear and non-linear relationships — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Gradient | Rate of change, calculating from two points, positive/negative/zero |
| Y-intercept | Identifying from equation and graph |
| Graphing linear equations | y = mx + b — drawing and reading graphs |
| Finding equations from graphs | Working backwards from a graph to its equation |
| Rate of change in context | Interpreting gradient in real-world situations |

**Open Math notes:** Strong candidate for a custom interactive renderer — an interactive coordinate plane where students plot lines and check their gradient and intercept. This topic bridges algebra and visual thinking.

---

### Surface Area and Volume
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Length, area and volume — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Surface area of prisms | Using nets, summing face areas |
| Surface area of cylinders | Curved surface (2πrh) + two circular ends (2πr²) |
| Volume of prisms | V = base area × height |
| Volume of cylinders | V = πr²h |
| Real-life applications | Capacity, material estimation, unit conversion |

**Open Math notes:** Formula-driven. Labelled 3D diagrams on sub-topic pages would significantly help. Good candidate for rendered SVG diagrams alongside default QuizRunner.

---

### Geometrical Properties
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Geometrical properties and figures — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Properties of triangles | Angle sum, types, exterior angles |
| Properties of quadrilaterals | Parallelograms, rhombuses, trapeziums — properties and angle relationships |
| Congruence | Four congruence tests (SSS, SAS, AAS, RHS) |
| Similarity | Scale factor, finding unknown sides in similar figures |

**Open Math notes:** Conceptual rather than formula-driven. Benefits from diagrams. Default renderer with embedded SVG shapes.

---

### Probability
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Probability — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Sample space | Listing outcomes, equally likely events |
| Theoretical probability | P(event) = favourable ÷ total, probability scale 0–1 |
| Experimental probability | Running trials, relative frequency, comparing to theoretical |
| Complementary events | P(A') = 1 − P(A) |
| Venn diagrams | Two events, intersection, union, mutually exclusive |

**Open Math notes:** High interactive potential — coin flip and dice simulators would make experimental probability genuinely engaging. Accessible entry point; lower anxiety than algebra topics.

---

### Statistics and Data
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Data classification, visualisation and analysis — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Types of data | Categorical vs numerical, discrete vs continuous |
| Measures of centre | Mean, median, mode — calculating and choosing the right one |
| Measures of spread | Range, interquartile range |
| Displaying data | Histograms, dot plots, stem-and-leaf plots |
| Interpreting data | Drawing conclusions, identifying skew, outliers |

**Open Math notes:** Lower anxiety topic, important for statistical literacy. Interpretation questions benefit from rendered chart components.

## Stage 5 Paths — Extension Content

Paths content is extension, taught at teacher's discretion. Listed here so the content tree can accommodate these topics when the time comes. They are not included in the Year 9 quiz by default.

| Topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Simultaneous equations | Substitution and elimination methods |
| Quadratic equations | Factorising, quadratic formula |
| Functions and graphs | Parabolas, hyperbolas, exponential graphs |
| Further trigonometry | Sine rule, cosine rule (non-right-angled triangles) |
| Further area and volume | Pyramids, cones, spheres |
| Geometrical proof | Formal proofs, introduction to circle geometry |
| Introduction to networks | Nodes, edges, paths, trees |
| Data analysis | Box plots, standard deviation, statistical investigations |
| Further probability | Tree diagrams, multi-stage events, conditional probability |
