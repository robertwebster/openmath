# Year 10 NSW Mathematics — Syllabus Map

**Syllabus:** Mathematics K–10 (2022), implemented in Year 10 from 2025  
**Stage:** Stage 5 (Years 9–10)  
**Structure:** Core (mandatory) + Paths (extension, noted separately)

> ⚠️ **Verification note:** Stage 5 spans both Year 9 and Year 10. NESA does not divide content by year — this mapping reflects the typical school teaching sequence where Year 9 covers introductory Stage 5 content and Year 10 covers the remaining and more advanced aspects. Confirm against your school's scope and sequence before building.

> ⚠️ **Overlap note:** Several Stage 5 Core focus areas were partially or fully covered in the Year 9 content set (Trigonometry A, Area and surface area A, Volume A, Properties of geometrical figures A, Data analysis A, Algebraic techniques A, Equations A, Linear relationships A, Indices A, Financial mathematics A — simple interest only). Year 10 does not duplicate that content. It covers what remains from those focus areas plus the B-level focus areas that are new to Year 10.

> ⚠️ **Surds note:** Surds are **not** part of the Stage 5 Core syllabus. They appear in Stage 5 Path content (Indices B/C). Schools that teach surds in Year 10 are delivering extension/Path content. The Core focus area "Numbers of any magnitude" covers significant figures, measurement error, and scientific notation — not surds.

---

## Navigation and Quiz Structure

The content is organised into **two navigable tiers** — Topic and Sub-topic. Strand is metadata only.

Three quiz levels exist, all drawing from a **single source of truth** at the sub-topic level:

| Level | What it covers | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **Sub-topic quiz** | One specific concept | "I need to practise depreciation" |
| **Topic quiz** | All sub-topics in a topic | "I think I've got non-linear relationships — test me" |
| **Year 10 quiz** | All topics in Year 10 | "I have an exam on everything" |

## Year 10 Topics

---

### Earning money
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Financial mathematics A — MA5-FIN-C-01

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Wages and overtime | Hourly rates; penalty rates for overtime, weekends and public holidays; calculating weekly and fortnightly pay |
| Leave loading and other income | Leave loading as a percentage of normal pay; piecework; royalties; calculating total earnings |
| Income tax and net pay | Gross vs net income; PAYG tax using published tables or online tools; allowable deductions; calculating annual tax and net earnings |

**Open Math notes:** Wages, leave loading and income tax were not built in the Year 9 content set (which covered simple interest, compound interest and budgeting only). This topic completes Financial mathematics A. Real-money contexts make these questions immediately relevant to students approaching working age. Income tax problems are a consistent source of exam errors — students confuse gross and net pay, and misread tax tables. Worked examples should show a full pay-slip calculation from gross pay to net pay.

### Investments and depreciation
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Financial mathematics B — MA5-FIN-C-02

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Compound interest | $FV = PV(1 + r)^n$; finding future value, present value or time period; comparing with simple interest |
| Depreciation | $S = V_0(1 - r)^n$; salvage value; connection between depreciation and compound interest formulas |
| Buying on terms and credit | Paying a deposit and making regular repayments; buy now pay later schemes; short-term loan costs |

**Open Math notes:** Compound interest was covered in Year 9 using $A = P(1 + r)^n$. Year 10 introduces the FV/PV notation used in Financial mathematics B and extends to depreciation. The depreciation formula $S = V_0(1 - r)^n$ mirrors the compound interest formula with $(1 - r)$ instead of $(1 + r)$ — make this structural parallel explicit in worked examples. Buying on terms (deposit plus regular repayments) and BNPL schemes are practical consumer contexts.

---

### Linear relationships
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Linear relationships B — MA5-LIN-C-02

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Gradient-intercept form | Interpreting $m$ and $c$ in $y = mx + c$; graphing using gradient and y-intercept; rate of change in context |
| Parallel lines | Identifying parallel lines from equations (equal gradients); writing equations of parallel lines through a given point |
| Perpendicular lines | Product of gradients rule ($m_1 \times m_2 = -1$); finding and verifying perpendicular gradients; writing equations of perpendicular lines |

**Open Math notes:** Gradient-intercept form was covered in Year 8 and Year 9. Linear relationships B adds two new concepts: equations of lines parallel to a given line, and the perpendicular gradient rule. The perpendicular gradient rule ($m_1 \times m_2 = -1$) is the one concept students find least intuitive — it needs concrete examples before the rule is stated. Finding the equation of a perpendicular through a given point is a reliable Year 10 exam question.

### Non-linear relationships
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Non-linear relationships A and B — MA5-NLI-C-01, MA5-NLI-C-02

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Parabolas | Features of $y = kx^2$ and $y = kx^2 + c$: vertex, axis of symmetry, x- and y-intercepts, concavity; effect of changing $k$ and $c$ |
| Exponential graphs | Features of $y = a^x$: y-intercept, asymptote, growth vs decay; behaviour for large and small values of $x$ |
| Comparing relationships | Distinguishing linear, quadratic and exponential from tables of values and graphs; recognising each type in real-life contexts; solving a linear and non-linear pair of equations graphically |

**Open Math notes:** Entirely new topic — no Year 9 foundation. This is the biggest conceptual jump in Year 10: students must recognise that not all relationships are straight lines. The focus in the Core syllabus is on graphing and identification, not algebraic manipulation (factorising trinomials is Path content). Questions should emphasise reading key features from graphs and matching equations to graphs rather than algebraic work. Graphing technology is explicitly mentioned in the NESA content descriptors.

### Bearings
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Trigonometry B — MA5-TRG-C-02

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| True bearings | Three-figure bearings measured clockwise from north (e.g. 045°, 270°); reading and stating bearings from diagrams |
| Compass bearings | Quadrant-style compass bearings (e.g. N45°E, S30°W); converting between true and compass bearings |
| Solving problems with bearings | Right-angled triangle problems involving bearings; finding distances and angles from bearing descriptions; two-leg journey problems |

**Open Math notes:** Angles of elevation and depression (the other content of Trigonometry B) were built in the Year 9 trigonometry topic. Bearings are the remaining new content here. Students consistently confuse true bearings (always three figures, clockwise from north) with compass bearings (quadrant notation). The conversion between the two systems needs to be a dedicated step in worked examples. Bearing problems require careful diagram construction before any trig can be applied.

### Measurement precision
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Numbers of any magnitude — MA5-MAG-C-01

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Significant figures | Rules for identifying and counting significant figures; rounding to a specified number of significant figures; effect of rounding on calculation results |
| Absolute and percentage error | Precision of a measuring instrument; absolute error = $\frac{1}{2} \times$ precision; percentage error = $\frac{\text{absolute error}}{\text{measurement}} \times 100\%$ |
| Scientific notation applications | Representing very large and very small numbers; ordering and comparing; estimating calculations using index laws; solving problems with scientific notation |

**Open Math notes:** Scientific notation was covered in Year 9 (index laws topic). This topic extends that with the new concepts of significant figures and measurement error. Note that this focus area is **not** about surds — that is a common misconception. The absolute/percentage error content is underemphasised in many Year 10 courses but appears in exams and is directly relevant to science subjects. The significant figures sub-topic connects to scientific literacy across the curriculum.

---

### Bivariate data
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Data analysis B — MA5-DAT-C-02

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Scatter plots and association | Constructing scatter plots for two numerical variables; describing association by form (linear), direction (positive, negative) and strength (strong, moderate, weak, none) |
| Line of best fit | Drawing a line of best fit by eye; distinguishing association from causation |
| Predictions and limitations | Using the line of best fit to interpolate and extrapolate; explaining why extrapolation is unreliable; identifying limitations of the model |

**Open Math notes:** Entirely new topic — no Year 9 foundation. Students often confuse "association" with "causation"; the distinction should be made explicit and revisited across all three sub-topics. The terminology for describing association (form, direction, strength) is specific and needs to be modelled clearly in worked examples — students who write "the graph goes up" instead of "there is a strong positive linear association" will not earn full marks. Interpolation vs extrapolation is a reliable exam question.

---

### Probability
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Probability A — MA5-PRB-C-01

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Independent and dependent events | Difference between independent and dependent events; effect of replacement on probability; identifying which type applies in a given context |
| Multiplying probabilities | Multiplication rule $P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \times P(B)$ for independent events; extending to dependent events using conditional probabilities; tree diagrams for two-stage experiments |
| Simulations | Designing and running a probability simulation using digital tools; using simulation results to estimate probabilities; evaluating how well a simulation models the real situation |

**Open Math notes:** Tree diagrams with and without replacement were covered in Year 8. Year 9 covered theoretical probability, experimental probability, complementary events and Venn diagrams. This topic formalises the distinction between independent and dependent events and introduces the multiplication rule. The simulation sub-topic is explicitly required by the NESA content descriptors and is often skipped in school teaching — it is a good candidate for a digital activity rather than a standard pen-and-paper quiz.

## Stage 5 Paths — Extension Content

These focus areas are Path (extension) content from the NSW 2022 Stage 5 syllabus. They are not part of the mandatory Core but are taught at most NSW schools, particularly those preparing students for Stage 6 Mathematics Advanced or Standard 2. They are not included in the Year 10 Core quiz by default.

| Topic | NESA focus area | Key content |
|---|---|---|
| Simultaneous equations | Equations B (Path) | Solving by substitution; solving by elimination; word problems requiring two variables |
| Quadratic expressions and equations | Algebraic techniques B/C (Path) | Expanding and factorising monic quadratic trinomials; difference of two squares; solving quadratic equations by factorising; null factor law |
| Surds | Indices B/C (Path) | Simplifying surds; adding and subtracting surds; multiplying and dividing surds; rationalising the denominator |
| Further linear relationships | Linear relationships C (Path) | Length and midpoint of an interval; distance formula; further analytical geometry |
| Further non-linear relationships | Non-linear relationships C (Path) | Sketching parabolas from factorised form; hyperbolas ($y = k/x$); circles ($x^2 + y^2 = r^2$) |
| Further trigonometry | Trigonometry C/D (Path) | Exact trig values (30°, 45°, 60°); sine rule; cosine rule; area of a triangle using $A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ |
| Further area and volume | Area and surface area B / Volume B (Path) | Surface area and volume of pyramids, cones and spheres |
| Circle geometry | Circle geometry (Path) | Circle theorems; angle properties of chords, tangents and secants |
| Introduction to networks | Introduction to networks (Path) | Nodes, edges, paths, trees; network problems |
| Further probability | Probability B (Path) | Conditional probability; Venn diagrams with three events |
| Further data analysis | Data analysis C (Path) | Standard deviation; statistical investigations |

## Build order suggestion

Priority topics (most commonly tested in Year 10 exams, most student anxiety):

1. **Non-linear relationships** — largest new concept, no prior foundation, required for Stage 6
2. **Earning money** — practical, accessible, builds student engagement
3. **Bivariate data** — entirely new, specific terminology needs early exposure
4. **Bearings** — builds on strong Year 9 trig base, contained topic
5. **Investments and depreciation** — extends Year 9 compound interest, natural follow-on
6. **Linear relationships** — extends known content, perpendicular lines is the key new idea
7. **Probability** — extends Year 8/9 base, simulation sub-topic may need digital tooling
8. **Measurement precision** — contained topic, lower anxiety, good for consolidation

Extension topics (if building Path content):

9. **Simultaneous equations** — prerequisite for Stage 6 content, taught at most schools
10. **Quadratic expressions and equations** — high exam weight at selective/advanced schools
11. Remaining Path topics in any order
