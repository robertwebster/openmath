# Year 11 NSW Mathematics Standard — Syllabus Map

**Syllabus:** Mathematics Standard 11–12 (2024), implemented from Term 1 2026  
**Stage:** Stage 6 — Mathematics Standard  
**Structure:** 8 focus areas covering Number, Measurement, Statistics, and Networks

> ⚠️ **Verification note:** Outcome codes (e.g. `MST-11-02`) should be confirmed against the official digital curriculum at [curriculum.nsw.edu.au](https://curriculum.nsw.edu.au/learning-areas/mathematics/mathematics-standard-11-12-2024/outcomes). The working outcome `MAO-WM-01` applies to all focus areas as the overarching working mathematically outcome.

---

## Navigation and Quiz Structure

The content is organised into **two navigable tiers** — Topic (= NESA focus area) and Sub-topic. Strand is metadata only.

Three quiz levels exist, all drawing from a **single source of truth** at the sub-topic level:

| Level | What it covers | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **Sub-topic quiz** | One specific concept | "I need to practise substituting into formulas" |
| **Topic quiz** | All sub-topics in a focus area | "I think I've got Earning Money — test me" |
| **Year 11 Standard quiz** | All focus areas | "I have an assessment on everything" |

Topic and Year quizzes are aggregated automatically from sub-topic question banks.

## Year 11 Standard Focus Areas

---

### Formulas and equations
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-01 (expected — verify)  
**Strand:** Number

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Substituting into formulas | Substitute values into linear and non-linear formulas; evaluate the subject; use spreadsheets |
| Distance, speed and time | DST formula problems; stopping distance formulas in practical contexts |
| BAC and medication formulas | BACmale and BACfemale formulas; Fried's, Young's and Clark's medication dosage formulas |
| Changing the subject | Change subject of linear and non-linear (quadratic form) formulas; represent word problems as equations |

**Open Math notes:** Strong real-world entry point for Stage 6. BAC and medication formulas are high-engagement, high-stakes contexts. Questions should include labelled formula sheets. Difficulty 3 questions should require students to rearrange non-linear formulas before substituting.

---

### Linear relationships
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-02  
**Strand:** Number / Algebra

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Linear modelling | Gradient, y-intercept; equation y=mx+c from graph or two points; construct graphs; interpret gradient as rate of change; identify limitations; spreadsheet modelling |
| Direct variation | y=kx through origin; find k from a pair of values; solve problems using the constant of variation |

**Open Math notes:** Revisits and extends Stage 5 linear relationships. Direct variation sub-topic introduces the idea of proportional modelling that feeds into Standard 2 and Standard 1 content. An interactive graphing tool on the sub-topic page would help significantly.

---

### Earning money
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-03  
**Strand:** Number / Financial Mathematics

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Wages and salaries | Salary; wages and overtime (time-and-a-half, double time, public holidays); leave loading; weekly/fortnightly/monthly/yearly conversions (1 year = 52 weeks) |
| Commission and other income | Flat and sliding-scale commission; bonuses; piecework; royalties; income as sole trader |
| Taxation | Taxable income and allowable deductions; PAYG tax tables; weekly/fortnightly/monthly tax withheld; net income after tax and Medicare levy; tax return — refund or payment owing |

**Open Math notes:** Among the most immediately relevant content for Year 11 students entering the workforce. Taxation questions require reading real-style tables — difficulty 3 questions should present a multi-step scenario combining earning and tax calculations. Spreadsheet tasks align well with worked examples.

---

### Managing money
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-04  
**Strand:** Number / Financial Mathematics

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Purchasing goods | Discounts, sale prices; GST and VAT; currency exchange (domestic and international); hire purchase (deposit + regular payments); compare purchasing methods |
| Vehicle costs | Purchase price or loan repayments; registration; CTP and comprehensive insurance; stamp duty at current rates; spreadsheet to model purchase costs |
| Budgeting | Ongoing vehicle running and maintenance costs; household bills (electricity, water, gas); personal budget (income vs fixed and variable expenses); emergency fund (3–6 months expenses) |

**Open Math notes:** Highly practical focus. Currency exchange questions need fresh data from ATO/RBA (questions should use representative but clearly hypothetical rates). Vehicle cost questions benefit from multi-part format. Difficulty 3 questions should involve comparing two purchasing strategies.

---

### Applications of measurement
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-05  
**Strand:** Measurement

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Metric units and conversions | Length (mm, cm, m, km); area (cm², m², ha, km²); volume (mm³, cm³, m³); capacity (mL, L, kL, ML); volume–capacity equivalences; mass (mg, g, kg, t); scientific notation with standard prefixes (nano to tera) |
| Perimeter and area | Triangles, rectangles, parallelograms, trapeziums, sectors, parts of circles, composite shapes; Pythagoras' theorem; irregular land by dissection |
| Volume and capacity | Prisms, cylinders, spheres, cones, pyramids, composite solids; Trapezoidal rule A ≈ h/2(d_f + d_l) for up to four applications |

**Open Math notes:** Formula-driven. Each sub-topic page should include a formula reference panel. Scientific notation prefix table (nano to tera) is new content in the 2024 syllabus — flag this clearly. Trapezoidal rule for area is a new addition; questions should include a diagram showing the trapezoids overlaid on a real shape.

---

### Time and location
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-06  
**Strand:** Measurement

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Latitude and longitude | Identify a location using latitude, longitude and hemisphere; 15° longitude = 1 hour time difference; calculate time differences from longitudes |
| Time zones and UTC | Convert between seconds, minutes, hours; 12-hour and 24-hour time; elapsed time; Australian time zones including daylight saving; UTC representation; solve problems involving UTC |

**Open Math notes:** Relatively compact focus area — suitable for two sub-topics. Difficulty 3 questions should involve a multi-city flight or event scenario. An interactive world map showing time zones would be high-value if feasible.

---

### Networks, paths and trees
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-07  
**Strand:** Number / Discrete Mathematics

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Network concepts | Define network (vertices and edges); terminology: vertex, edge, degree, loop, connected graph; weighted and directed edges; real-world networks; construct network diagrams from tables or maps |
| Shortest paths and spanning trees | Define path, walk, tree, spanning tree, minimum spanning tree (MST); find MST using any method; minimal connector problems; identify shortest path on networks with ≤ 10 vertices; discuss when shortest path ≠ MST |

**Open Math notes:** Network diagrams cannot be rendered as text — this topic will need a diagrammatic question format (images or interactive graphs). This is a completely new topic for many students (no Year 7–10 equivalent). Difficulty 1 should focus on reading and interpreting diagrams; difficulty 3 on applying algorithms and justifying conclusions.

---

### Data analysis
**NESA outcome:** MST-11-08  
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Statistical investigation and sampling | Pose statistical question; survey design principles (clear language, unambiguous questions); sampling methods (systematic, self-selected, random, stratified); representative samples; privacy, bias, ethics |
| Displaying data | Classify data (numerical/categorical, discrete/continuous); frequency and relative frequency tables; histograms with polygon; dot plots, line graphs, sector graphs, divided bar charts; shape of distribution (symmetric, positively/negatively skewed); identify misleading representations |
| Measures of centre and spread | Mean, median, mode; population standard deviation σ; uniform/unimodal/bimodal/multimodal; compare datasets; choose appropriate measure for context |
| Quartiles, box plots and outliers | Quartiles Q1, Q2, Q3; IQR; five-number summary; box plots; parallel box plots; identify outliers using Q1−1.5×IQR and Q3+1.5×IQR; impact of outliers on measures |

**Open Math notes:** The most content-rich focus area. Standard deviation is now required (was extension in the 2017 syllabus). Box plots and outlier detection are assessable. Difficulty 3 questions should involve interpreting and comparing two datasets from parallel box plots or histograms. A rendered chart component would be valuable here.

## Suggested Build Order

Build order below balances curriculum dependencies, student engagement, and Open Math development priorities:

| Priority | Focus area | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Earning money | Most immediately relevant; clear formula-and-table structure |
| 2 | Formulas and equations | Foundation for all other topics; accessible entry |
| 3 | Linear relationships | Extends Stage 5 knowledge; small scope |
| 4 | Managing money | Highly practical; builds on earning money |
| 5 | Data analysis | Rich content; most questions are calculable |
| 6 | Applications of measurement | Formula-driven; can reuse patterns from Year 9/10 |
| 7 | Time and location | Compact; useful as a confidence-builder |
| 8 | Networks, paths and trees | Needs custom diagram support — build infrastructure first |
