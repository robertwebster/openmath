# Year 11 NSW Mathematics Advanced — Syllabus Map

**Syllabus:** Mathematics Advanced 11–12 (2024), implemented from Term 1 2026  
**Stage:** Stage 6 — Mathematics Advanced  
**Structure:** 7 focus areas — abstract and proof-oriented, calculus begins in Year 11

> ⚠️ **Verification note:** Outcome codes (e.g. `MAV-11-04`) should be confirmed against the official digital curriculum at [curriculum.nsw.edu.au](https://curriculum.nsw.edu.au/learning-areas/mathematics/mathematics-advanced-11-12-2024/outcomes). The working outcome `MAO-WM-01` applies to all focus areas.

---

## Navigation and Quiz Structure

The content is organised into **two navigable tiers** — Topic (= NESA focus area) and Sub-topic. Strand is metadata only.

Three quiz levels exist, all drawing from a **single source of truth** at the sub-topic level:

| Level | What it covers | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **Sub-topic quiz** | One specific concept | "I need to practise the chain rule" |
| **Topic quiz** | All sub-topics in a focus area | "I think I've got Differentiation — test me" |
| **Year 11 Advanced quiz** | All focus areas | "I have a half-yearly on everything" |

Topic and Year quizzes are aggregated automatically from sub-topic question banks.

## Year 11 Advanced Focus Areas

### Working with functions
**NESA outcomes:** MAV-11-01, MAV-11-02 (expected — verify)  
**Strand:** Algebra and Functions

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Algebraic techniques | Index laws; expanding and factorising; surds and rationalising denominators; quadratic formula; discriminant (nature of roots) |
| Introduction to functions | Functions vs relations; vertical line test; domain, range, function notation f(x); zeroes; even and odd functions |
| Linear and quadratic functions | Gradient-intercept, general form, point-gradient; parallel/perpendicular lines; factored form of quadratic; completing the square; axis of symmetry and vertex; graphing parabolas; simultaneous linear and quadratic |
| Cubic and reciprocal functions | Cubic functions (factored form, x-intercepts); reciprocal function f(x)=k/x; hyperbola graph; asymptotes |
| Properties of functions | Composite functions f(g(x)); piecewise-defined functions (graphing, continuity, discontinuities); absolute value functions; graph y=|x|; solve |ax+b|=k |
| Variation and modelling | Direct variation y=kxⁿ; inverse variation y=k/xⁿ; find k from data; simultaneous equations for constructing models; real-world function modelling |

**Open Math notes:** The most substantial focus area — covers all the function types needed for the rest of the course. Build sub-topics in the order listed as each builds on the last. Absolute value is commonly assessed in HSC exams. Piecewise functions are entirely new to most students. Interactive graphing tools (Desmos-style embed) would be high-value here.

### Trigonometry and measure of angles
**NESA outcome:** MAV-11-04  
**Strand:** Functions / Trigonometry

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Trig with acute angles | Exact ratios for 30°, 45°, 60° using Pythagoras; apply trig to right-angled triangles; bearings; angles of elevation and depression |
| Non-right-angled triangles | Sine rule a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC; cosine rule c²=a²+b²−2ab cosC; area formula A=½ab sinC; ambiguous case (two possible triangles) |
| Trigonometry with any angle | Represent angles in Cartesian plane; define cosθ=x/r, sinθ=y/r, tanθ=y/x; evaluate for multiples of 90°; ratios for 180°±A, 360°−A; cos(−θ)=cosθ, sin(−θ)=−sinθ, tan(−θ)=−tanθ |
| Radians | Define radian as arc length / radius; 360°=2π; convert degrees↔radians; exact trig for multiples of π/6 and π/4; graph y=sinx, y=cosx, y=tanx; arc length l=rθ; sector area A=½r²θ; segments and composite problems |

**Open Math notes:** The ambiguous case requires a diagram — flagged as needing image support. Radians must be introduced carefully: students need to understand why the definition works before applying formulas. Graph of trig functions (period, amplitude, domain, range) is taught here — these graphs recur throughout Year 12. Difficulty 3 questions should combine sine/cosine rule with area in multi-step problems.

### Trigonometric identities and equations
**NESA outcome:** MAV-11-05  
**Strand:** Functions / Trigonometry

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Reciprocal trig ratios | Define sec θ, cosec θ, cot θ for acute angles; exact values for 30°, 45°, 60°; unit circle definitions; reciprocal identities: secA=1/cosA, cosecA=1/sinA, cotA=1/tanA; exact values for multiples of π/6 and π/4 |
| Trigonometric identities | tan θ=sinθ/cosθ; cot θ=cosθ/sinθ; complementary angle identities (sin(90°−θ)=cosθ, etc.); Pythagorean identities: cos²x+sin²x=1, 1+tan²x=sec²x, 1+cot²x=cosec²x |
| Trigonometric equations | Evaluate trig expressions using any-angle identities; solve equations involving trig ratios on a restricted domain; equations reducing to quadratics; prove identities by substitution or reducing to sinx and cosx |

**Open Math notes:** Proof-based content — this is the first focus area requiring formal mathematical proof. Questions requiring students to "show that" are distinct from "find" questions and need a different marking approach. Difficulty 3 questions should involve multi-step proofs or solving equations with two trig functions simultaneously.

### Introduction to differentiation
**NESA outcome:** MAV-11-06  
**Strand:** Calculus

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Rates of change | Average rate of change Δy/Δx = [f(b)−f(a)]/(b−a) as gradient of secant; average speed from distance-time graph; instantaneous speed as limit of average speed; instantaneous rate of change for non-linear functions; estimate gradient from graph |
| The derivative | Gradient of curve at a point; approximate using secant through P and Q as h→0; infer d/dx(xⁿ)=nxⁿ⁻¹; define f'(x) as gradient of tangent; first principles f'(x)=lim[h→0] [f(x+h)−f(x)]/h; first principles for quadratic functions |
| Differentiation rules | Notation dy/dx, y', d/dx f(x); d/dx(xⁿ)=nxⁿ⁻¹ for all real n; sum rule; scalar multiple rule; product rule; quotient rule; chain rule; find gradients of tangents and normals; points where tangent has given gradient; angle of inclination tan θ=m |
| Applications of the derivative | Increasing (f'(c)>0) and decreasing (f'(c)<0); stationary points (f'(c)=0); graph y=f'(x) from y=f(x); stationary points and monotonicity of cubic functions; interpret f'(c) as instantaneous rate of change; displacement vs distance; velocity vs speed; velocity as dx/dt |

**Open Math notes:** Calculus begins in Year 11 Advanced — a significant step up from Stage 5. First principles must be taught before rules are introduced. Product, quotient and chain rules are all assessable in Year 11. Difficulty 1 questions should focus on applying d/dx(xⁿ); difficulty 3 questions should combine chain rule with finding tangent equations or solving rate-of-change problems.

### Exponential and logarithmic functions
**NESA outcomes:** MAV-11-07, MAV-11-08  
**Strand:** Functions / Calculus

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Exponential functions | Graph y=kaˣ and y=ka⁻ˣ; asymptote, y-intercept, domain, range, behaviour as x→±∞; examine gradient of y=aˣ at y-intercept for varying a; Euler's number e≈2.71828 as the base where gradient = 1 at x=0; d/dx(eˣ)=eˣ |
| Logarithmic functions | Define logₐy as index to which a is raised to give y; natural logarithm ln a = logₑa; equivalence y=aˣ ↔ x=logₐy; solve aˣ=b; laws: logₐ(mn), logₐ(m/n), logₐ(mⁿ); results: logₐa=1, logₐ1=0, logₐ(1/x)=−logₐx; change of base: logₐx=logbx/logba; graph y=logₐx; y=aˣ and y=logₐx are reflections in y=x |

**Open Math notes:** Euler's number e is introduced here — students need to understand it emerges from the uniqueness of the derivative condition, not just as a number to memorise. The connection between exponentials and logarithms (inverse functions) should be emphasised visually. Difficulty 3 questions should involve solving equations using log laws or combining with other function types.

### Graph transformations
**NESA outcome:** MAV-11-03  
**Strand:** Functions / Algebra

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Reflections and translations | Replace x by −x → reflect in y-axis; replace y by −y → reflect in x-axis; replace x by x−a → horizontal translation by a; replace y by y−b → vertical translation by b; sign of a and b determines direction |
| Dilations and combined transformations | Replace x by x/k → horizontal dilation by factor k; replace y by y/l → vertical dilation by factor l; combined dilation (enlargement k>1, reduction 0<k<1); order of transformations matters; apply to all Advanced functions (excluding trig); determine domain, range, intercepts, asymptotes of transformed graph |
| Circles | (x−a)²+(y−b)²=r² — centre (a,b), radius r, domain and range; x²+y²+ax+by+c=0 — complete the square to find centre and radius; graph circles and semicircles; find equation from graph |

**Open Math notes:** The "order of transformations matters" point is commonly a source of student error and should be tested explicitly. Circles extend the function/relation content from Working with Functions. Difficulty 3 questions should require students to apply a sequence of transformations and state the resulting equation and key features.

### Probability and data
**NESA outcomes:** MAV-11-09, MAV-11-10  
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Sets and Venn diagrams | Set notation (elements, n(A), ∅, complement, subset, intersection, union); disjoint sets; Venn diagrams in practical contexts; inclusion-exclusion: |A∪B|=|A|+|B|−|A∩B| |
| Probability rules | Sample space; P(A)=|A|/|S| for equally likely outcomes; complement P(Ā)=1−P(A); addition rule P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B); mutually exclusive events; counting using arrays, tables and tree diagrams |
| Conditional probability | Define P(A|B); restrict sample space using Venn diagrams, tables and tree diagrams; P(A|B)=P(A∩B)/P(B); independent events P(A|B)=P(A); equivalence: independence ↔ P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B); solve practical problems |
| Data and random variables | Define random variable; discrete vs continuous variables; tabulate datasets (frequency, relative frequency, cumulative relative frequency); graph frequency and cumulative frequency; use relative frequency to estimate probability |

**Open Math notes:** Set notation is formally introduced here — many students encounter it for the first time. Venn diagrams bridge from Standard-style intuition to formal probability. Conditional probability is the most conceptually demanding sub-topic; the restriction of sample space interpretation is essential before introducing the formula. Difficulty 3 questions should combine conditional probability with counting or require students to prove independence.

## Suggested Build Order

| Priority | Focus area | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Working with functions | Prerequisite for all other focus areas |
| 2 | Trigonometry and measure of angles | High exam weighting; builds on Stage 5 trig |
| 3 | Exponential and logarithmic functions | Foundational for Year 12 calculus of e and ln |
| 4 | Introduction to differentiation | Core calculus — large scope, high HSC weighting |
| 5 | Trigonometric identities and equations | Builds on focus area 2; proof content |
| 6 | Probability and data | Mostly self-contained; manageable scope |
| 7 | Graph transformations | Enhances all other function types; good late-course consolidation |

## Relationship to Mathematics Standard

Mathematics Advanced covers all content in Mathematics Standard plus significantly more:

- Advanced adds full calculus (differentiation from first principles, product/quotient/chain rules)
- Advanced requires proofs and formal identity work (trig identities)
- Advanced treats functions formally (domain, range, composition, inverse)
- Standard focuses on practical numeracy (taxation, budgeting, measurement applications, networks)
- Standard and Advanced students share some content (trig with acute angles, data analysis, probability basics) but at different depths

When building Open Math Year 11, the two tracks should be clearly separated with a course selector on the Year 11 landing page.
