# Year 8 NSW Mathematics — Syllabus Map

**Syllabus:** Mathematics K–10 (2022), implemented in Year 8 from 2024  
**Stage:** Stage 4 (Years 7–8) — advanced content  
**Structure:** All topics are core (mandatory for Stage 4)

> ⚠️ **Verification note:** Stage 4 spans both Year 7 and Year 8. The NESA syllabus does not divide content by year — this mapping reflects the typical school teaching sequence where Year 7 covers introductory Stage 4 content and Year 8 covers the more advanced aspects. Confirm against your school's scope and sequence before building.

> ⚠️ **Overlap note:** Some Stage 4 content (surface area of prisms/cylinders, volume of cylinders, congruence, similarity) was placed in the Year 9 content set during initial build. Year 8 does not duplicate those — it covers the remaining advanced Stage 4 content.

---

## Navigation and Quiz Structure

The content is organised into **two navigable tiers** — Topic and Sub-topic. Strand is metadata only.

Three quiz levels exist, all drawing from a **single source of truth** at the sub-topic level:

| Level | What it covers | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **Sub-topic quiz** | One specific concept | "I need to practise percentage increase" |
| **Topic quiz** | All sub-topics in a topic | "I think I've got linear equations — test me" |
| **Year 8 quiz** | All topics in Year 8 | "I have an exam on everything" |

## Year 8 Topics

---

### Fractions, Decimals and Percentages
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Fractions, decimals and percentages — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Percentage increase and decrease | Increase/decrease by a percentage; finding the new amount; reverse percentages |
| Profit, loss and discounts | Profit and loss as percentages of cost price; discount and sale price; GST |
| Fraction operations | Adding and subtracting fractions with unlike denominators; multiplying and dividing fractions; mixed numerals |

**Open Math notes:** Percentage problems are the most common source of exam errors in Year 8. Reverse percentages (finding the original amount) need dedicated treatment — students routinely confuse "find 20% less" with "find the original if the result is 20% less". Real-money contexts (sale prices, GST) make these sticky.

### Ratios and Rates
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Ratios and rates — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Dividing in a ratio | Sharing a quantity in a given ratio; finding amounts when one part is known |
| Scale and maps | Reading and constructing scale drawings; calculating real distances from scale |
| Speed, distance and time | $s = d/t$ and rearrangements; unit conversions; average speed problems |

**Open Math notes:** Builds directly on Year 7 ratios and rates. Speed-distance-time is the most tested rate application in Years 8–9. Scale and maps problems appear regularly in NAPLAN-style questions.

### Algebraic Techniques
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Algebraic techniques — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Simplifying algebraic fractions | Adding, subtracting, multiplying and dividing algebraic fractions; cancelling common factors |
| Expanding and simplifying | Expanding two brackets (binomials); simplifying by collecting like terms; mixed expressions |
| Factorising expressions | Identifying and extracting the HCF; factorising two-term and three-term expressions with a common factor |

**Open Math notes:** Algebraic fractions are a major conceptual jump from Year 7. Many students attempt to cancel incorrectly (e.g. cancelling terms rather than factors). Factorising here is HCF only — trinomial factorisation is Stage 5 (Year 9). Distinguish this clearly in worked examples.

### Equations
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Equations — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Multi-step equations | Equations with variables on both sides; expanding brackets before solving; checking solutions |
| Equations with fractions | Equations where the unknown is in the numerator of a fraction; multiplying through to clear fractions |
| Word problems | Translating worded scenarios into equations; defining variables; interpreting the solution |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 covered one-step and two-step equations. Year 8 adds variables on both sides and fraction equations. Word problems are consistently the hardest question type in school exams — students benefit from seeing a clear "let x = ..." setup modelled in every worked example.

---

### Linear Relationships
**Strand:** Number and Algebra  
**NESA focus area:** Linear relationships — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Gradient and y-intercept | Calculating gradient from two points; identifying y-intercept; gradient-intercept form $y = mx + b$ |
| Equation of a line | Finding the equation of a line from a point and gradient, or from two points |
| Sketching linear equations | Plotting lines using gradient and intercept; using intercepts to sketch; horizontal and vertical lines |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 introduced plotting points and identifying linear patterns. Year 8 formalises gradient, intercept, and the equation of a line. This feeds directly into simultaneous equations (Year 9). Negative gradients and fraction gradients need extra attention. Horizontal and vertical lines (undefined gradient, zero gradient) are a regular exam trip-wire.

### Area
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Area — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Area of circles | $A = \pi r^2$; given radius or diameter; composite shapes |
| Area of special quadrilaterals | Parallelogram ($A = bh$), trapezium ($A = \frac{1}{2}(a+b)h$), rhombus and kite ($A = \frac{1}{2}d_1 d_2$) |
| Composite shapes with circles | Semicircles, quarter-circles, annuli; adding and subtracting areas |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 covered rectangles, triangles, and composite rectilinear shapes. Year 8 adds circles and the four special quadrilaterals. The rhombus/kite diagonal formula is the one students most often forget — it appears in every Year 8 exam. Composite shapes with circles require students to decide whether to add or subtract the circular portion.

---

### Volume
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Volume — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Volume of cylinders | $V = \pi r^2 h$; given radius or diameter; real-world contexts (tanks, pipes, cans) |
| Composite volumes | Combining a cylinder with a prism; solving for an unknown dimension given the volume |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 covered volume of prisms ($V = Ah$). Cylinders are the natural next step — the same formula applies with $A = \pi r^2$. Make this connection explicit in worked examples. Note: surface area of cylinders is handled in the Year 9 content set.

### Angle Relationships
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Angle relationships — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Angles in polygons | Interior angle sum of polygons ($S = (n-2) \times 180°$); exterior angles; regular polygons |
| Parallel lines and transversals | More complex multi-step problems; identifying angle relationships in composite diagrams; algebraic angle problems |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 introduced types of angles and basic parallel line properties (co-interior, alternate, corresponding). Year 8 adds the polygon angle sum formula and more complex parallel line reasoning. Algebraic angle problems (e.g. "find x given the diagram") are a staple of Year 8 exams.

### Properties of Geometrical Figures
**Strand:** Measurement and Space  
**NESA focus area:** Properties of geometrical figures — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Reflections and rotations | Reflecting figures across horizontal, vertical, and diagonal axes; rotating figures about the origin |
| Translations and dilations | Translating figures using vector notation; dilating figures from a centre of dilation; scale factor |

**Open Math notes:** Transformations are Stage 4 content not covered in Year 7. Dilations (scale factor) are the most challenging transformation and connect to similar figures in Year 9. Note: congruence and formal similarity proofs are handled in the Year 9 geometrical-properties content set.

### Data Classification and Visualisation
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Data classification and visualisation — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Histograms and frequency polygons | Constructing and interpreting histograms from grouped data; frequency polygons; class width and midpoint |
| Back-to-back stem plots | Constructing and reading back-to-back stem-and-leaf plots; comparing two data sets visually |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 covered types of data and basic displays (dot plots, line graphs, sector graphs). Year 8 introduces histograms and back-to-back stem plots, which are the Year 8 exam staples. The key skill is interpreting — reading off medians, modes, and shapes of distribution — not just drawing.

---

### Data Analysis
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Data analysis — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Five-number summary and box plots | Minimum, Q1, median, Q3, maximum; constructing and reading box plots; outliers |
| Comparing data sets | Using mean, median, range, and IQR to compare two data sets; interpreting back-to-back displays |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 covered measures of centre (mean, median, mode) and range. Year 8 introduces the five-number summary and box plots, which are the standard Year 8–9 data display. Comparing data sets requires students to make statements using statistical language — model this explicitly in worked examples.

---

### Probability
**Strand:** Statistics and Probability  
**NESA focus area:** Probability — *verify outcome code*

| Sub-topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Multi-stage experiments | Listing the sample space for two-step experiments; counting outcomes; calculating probabilities |
| Tree diagrams | Constructing tree diagrams for two-stage experiments; reading probabilities from branches; with and without replacement |

**Open Math notes:** Year 7 covered simple theoretical and experimental probability. Year 8 adds two-stage experiments and tree diagrams. "With replacement" vs "without replacement" is the critical distinction. Note: Venn diagrams and complementary events are in the Year 9 content set.

## Build order suggestion

Priority topics (most commonly tested in Year 8 exams, most student anxiety):

1. **Equations** (multi-step, with fractions, word problems) — biggest jump from Year 7
2. **Linear relationships** (gradient, equation of a line) — feeds Year 9 simultaneous equations
3. **Area** (circles, special quadrilaterals) — high formula recall demand
4. **Fractions, decimals and percentages** (percentage applications, fraction operations)
5. **Algebraic techniques** (simplifying algebraic fractions, factorising)
6. Remaining topics in any order
