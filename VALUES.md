# Open Math — Project Values

These values exist to keep the project honest. When a decision feels hard, come back here first.

---

## 1. Radical Accessibility

No accounts. No subscriptions. No premium tier. The moment a student needs to sign up or pay to access the good stuff, the project has failed its purpose.

Everything lives in the open — open source code, open access content, open to fork and self-host. A student in rural NSW with a school laptop and a slow connection should get the same experience as anyone else.

## 2. Curriculum-First, Not Content-First

Every piece of content must trace back to a specific NSW syllabus outcome. The goal is not to have a lot of material — it is to have the *right* material. A Year 8 student preparing for a topic test should be able to find exactly what they are being assessed on, nothing more and nothing less.

The NSW Mathematics syllabus is the source of truth. If a topic isn't in the syllabus, it isn't in the project.

## 3. Instant Feedback Over Passive Learning

Static notes are not enough. A student should be able to attempt something, receive feedback, and know where they stand — without needing a teacher or parent in the room. That feedback loop is the core of the experience.

Where possible, feedback should be specific: not just "incorrect" but an indication of *where* the working went wrong or *which concept* to revisit.

## 4. Confidence as the Metric

The measure of success is not whether a student got the right answer. It is whether they feel less scared of a topic than they did ten minutes ago.

This shapes everything: the tone of feedback, the difficulty curve within a topic, how errors are handled, and what counts as progress. A student who attempted three hard questions and got two wrong but understands why has had a successful session.

## 5. AI-Assisted, Human-Curated Content — and Honest About It

The project does not rely on a volunteer community to produce content. Questions, worked examples, and explanations are generated with AI assistance, working against the NSW syllabus as the brief. A human makes the final call on quality, accuracy, and tone before anything is published.

This keeps the content pipeline realistic for a small team or a single maintainer, without pretending that a contributor community will appear.

We do not hide this. The site will say plainly that content is AI-generated and syllabus-reviewed. At a time when many platforms quietly use AI while presenting content as hand-crafted, transparency is the more trustworthy position — and the more honest one.

## 6. Open Ethos by Default

Features like user accounts, gamification, leaderboards, and monetisation are not inherently wrong — but each one must be a deliberate decision argued against these values, not something that drifts in because it seemed like a good idea at the time.

Session storage may be used to preserve a student's progress within a visit. Persistent accounts will not be introduced. The project will not accept sponsorship or advertising that compromises the independence of the content.

---

*These values were written at the start of the project. They should be revisited, not ignored, as the project grows.*
